Study Abroad FAQs

What are the general admission requirements for studying abroad?
Most universities require academic transcripts, standardized test scores (SAT, ACT, GRE, GMAT), an English proficiency test (IELTS, TOEFL, PTE), a Statement of Purpose (SOP), Letters of Recommendation (LORs), and a resume/CV.
Which English proficiency test should I take?
USA, UK, Australia, Ireland: IELTS, TOEFL, PTE, Duolingo (some universities). France, Germany: Some universities accept English-taught programs without IELTS if you have prior education in English.
What is the cost of studying abroad?
  • USA: $20,000 - $60,000 per year
  • UK: £10,000 - £35,000 per year
  • Australia: AUD 20,000 - AUD 45,000 per year
  • France: €3,000 - €30,000 per year
  • Germany: Free or €1,500 per semester (public universities)
  • Ireland: €9,000 - €25,000 per year
Can I work while studying abroad?
  • USA: Up to 20 hours/week on-campus during semesters.
  • UK: Up to 20 hours/week during semesters and full-time during holidays.
  • Germany: 120 full days or 240 half days per year.
  • Australia: Unlimited work rights for international students.
  • France: 964 hours/year (20 hours per week).
  • Ireland: Up to 20 hours/week during semesters, full-time during holidays.
What are the best scholarships for international students?
Many countries offer scholarships such as Fulbright (USA), Chevening (UK), DAAD (Germany), Eiffel (France), and Australia Awards (Australia).
Is health insurance mandatory for international students?
Yes, most countries require health insurance for student visas. Some universities offer health plans, while others require private insurance.
