Université PSL (Paris Sciences et Lettres)

Université PSL (Paris Sciences et Lettres) is a prestigious research university located in Paris, France, established in 2010 as a merger of several renowned institutions. It is renowned for its excellence in a wide range of disciplines, including humanities, social sciences, natural sciences, and engineering. PSL boasts a strong international reputation, attracting top researchers and students from around the world. The university fosters interdisciplinary collaboration and is recognized for its commitment to innovation, academic rigor, and its contributions to global research.

  • Opportunities: Scholarships, research programs, and work-study opportunities available for international students.
  • Application Process:
    • Select the program you wish to apply for, whether undergraduate, master's, or doctoral studies. Make sure to check the specific requirements for your chosen program.
    • Ensure that you meet the academic qualifications and language proficiency requirements for the program. For many programs, proficiency in French or English is required, depending on the language of instruction.
    • Prepare necessary documents, such as academic transcripts, CV, motivation letter, proof of language proficiency (e.g., IELTS, TOEFL for English or DELF, DALF for French), recommendation letters, and passport copy.
    • Depending on the program, you may be required to attend an interview or submit additional materials, such as a portfolio for creative programs or a research proposal for doctoral programs.
    • The tuition fees for undergraduate programs for EU students are generally around €170 per year. For non-EU students, the fees are higher and can range from €2,770 to €3,770 per year
    • The fees for master's programs for EU students are typically around €243 per year. The fees for non-EU students can range from €3,770 to €6,000 per year, depending on the program and the specific school within PSL.
  • Eligibility Criteria:
    • Completion of secondary education (high school diploma or equivalent), proficiency in French (DELF/TCF) for French-taught programs, or English (TOEFL/IELTS) for English-taught programs.
    • Completion of a Bachelor's degree (or equivalent), proficiency in French (B2 level) for French-taught programs, or English proficiency (TOEFL/IELTS) for English-taught programs, and some programs may require a motivation letter, CV, or portfolio.
    • Completion of a Master's degree (or equivalent), proficiency in French or English, and may require a research proposal, CV, and possibly an interview.
  • Important links:
    • For detailed application information, visit the: Université PSL (Paris Sciences et Lettres)
  • Status: Currently closed for the January session.

Université Sorbonne

Université Sorbonne, located in Paris, is one of the oldest and most prestigious universities in France, with a rich history dating back to the 13th century. It offers a wide range of academic programs across humanities, social sciences, natural sciences, and arts. The university is renowned for its research output, particularly in literature, history, and philosophy, and fosters international collaborations with universities around the world. With a vibrant student body, it provides a dynamic learning environment in the heart of one of the world’s cultural capitals.

  • Opportunities: Numerous scholarships available, including merit-based scholarships and financial assistance for international students.
  • Application Process:
    • Select the program you wish to apply for (Bachelor’s, Master’s, or Doctorate) and check the specific entry requirements.
    • For undergraduate: A high school diploma (or equivalent). For graduate: A recognized Bachelor's degree (for Master’s) or Master's degree (for Doctorate). Language proficiency: French (for French-taught programs) or English (for English-taught programs).
    • Collect necessary documents, including: Academic transcripts and certificates, Proof of language proficiency (DELF/DALF for French, TOEFL/IELTS for English), Motivation letter, CV, or research proposal (depending on the program), Passport copy and photo.
    • Apply online through the university’s admissions platform or via Campus France for international students.
    • Once your application is submitted, wait for the university’s decision, which may include an interview or further documentation requests.
    • Undergraduate Programs: For EU/EEA students: Tuition fees are generally around 170 EUR per year. For non-EU students: Tuition fees can range from 2,770 EUR to 3,770 EUR per year, depending on the program.
    • Master’s Programs: For EU/EEA students: Tuition fees are typically 243 EUR per year. For non-EU students: Fees range from 3,770 EUR to 5,000 EUR per year, depending on the program.
    • Doctoral Programs: For EU/EEA students: Tuition fees are around 380 EUR per year. For non-EU students: Fees are usually between 3,770 EUR to 5,000 EUR per year.
  • Eligibility Criteria:
    • Completion of secondary education (high school diploma or equivalent), proficiency in French (DELF/TCF) for French-taught programs, or English (TOEFL/IELTS) for English-taught programs.
    • Completion of a Bachelor's degree (or equivalent), proficiency in French (B2 level) for French-taught programs, or English proficiency (TOEFL/IELTS) for English-taught programs, and some programs may require a motivation letter, CV, or portfolio.
    • Completion of a Master's degree (or equivalent), proficiency in French or English, and may require a research proposal, CV, and possibly an interview.
  • Important links:
    • For detailed application information, visit the: Université Sorbonne
  • Status: Applications open for Semester 1 intake (March) and Semester 2 intake (July).

HEC Paris

HEC Paris is one of the leading business schools in Europe, renowned for its rigorous academic programs and strong global reputation. It offers a range of programs, including an MBA, Executive Education, and a highly regarded undergraduate business degree. Located near Paris, the school has a strong emphasis on leadership, entrepreneurship, and strategic management, with a global network of alumni and business connections. HEC Paris is consistently ranked among the top business schools in the world, attracting students and executives from diverse backgrounds.

  • Opportunities: HEC Paris offers a variety of scholarships and financial support options for international students.
  • Application Process:
    • Complete the online application on the HEC Paris website.
    • Required Documents: Academic transcripts and certificates, GMAT/GRE scores (for certain programs), TOEFL/IELTS/PTE scores (for non-native English speakers), CV/Resume, Motivation letter/Essays, Letters of recommendation (typically two), Passport copy, & Application fee payment (if applicable)
    • Shortlisted candidates will be invited for an interview, which may be in person or online.
    • Successful applicants will receive an offer letter to join the program.
    • Approximately €78,000 for the entire duration of the program.
  • Eligibility Criteria:
    • Completion of secondary education (high school diploma or equivalent), proficiency in French (DELF/TCF) for French-taught programs, or English (TOEFL/IELTS) for English-taught programs.
    • Completion of a Bachelor's degree (or equivalent), proficiency in French (B2 level) for French-taught programs, or English proficiency (TOEFL/IELTS) for English-taught programs, and some programs may require a motivation letter, CV, or portfolio.
    • Completion of a Master's degree (or equivalent), proficiency in French or English, and may require a research proposal, CV, and possibly an interview.
  • Important links:
    • For detailed application information, visit the: HEC Paris
  • Status: The application deadlines vary, but generally the intake is in September with rounds typically starting in October and ending by April.
